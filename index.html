HISTORY OF ARCHERY

OLYMPICS

In 1900, 1904, 1908, and 1920, archery was a part of the Olympic program. After a 52-year break, it made a comeback in 1972 and has been a part of the show ever since. Recurve bows are used by competitors in target archery competitions at the Olympics. Based on early results, Hubert van Innis of Belgium and Kim Soo-Nyung of Korea are the two most successful Olympians in the sport of archery. The only archer to have won the individual Olympic title twice is Darrell Pace of the USA. The country with the most Olympic success in archery is Korea.

PARALYMPICS

Archery has featured on the programme of the Paralympic Games since its first edition in 1960 and is rooted in the history of the Paralympic movement, having been used by Dr. Ludwig Gutmann as a rehabilitation activity at Stoke Mandeville Hospital in England, where early versions of the event were held. Impaired athletes compete in para archery events with recurve and compound bows at the Paralympics.

WORLD GAMES

Since 1985, archery has been a part of the World Games schedule. Before the compound bow was introduced in 1995, field archery competitions were only open to recurve and barebow archers. The target archery discipline was added to the compound competition in 2013. The compound men's field archery event was won three times by Morgan Lundin, who is the most successful archer at the World Games in 1993, 1997, and 2005.

TYPE OF BOWS

A bow consists of a semi-rigid but elastic arc with a high-tensile bowstring joining the ends of the two limbs of the bow. An arrow is a projectile with a projected tip and a long shaft with fins (fletching) towards the back, with a narrow notch (nock) at the very end to contact the bowstring.

Recurve or Classical bow

The curved branches and more massive handle of the recurve bow make it easy to identify. It can be used instinctively without a sighting device, but attachments like a stabilizer, a sight, and vibration dampers can be enhanced. It comes in a variety of shapes and weights, up to 40 pounds for adult recreational shooting.

Straight or Longbow

Another common traditional bow is the longbow, which is big (2 meters), straight, and D-shaped. Depending on your morphology, straight bows can now be found in a variety of sizes. It is constructed from a single block of yew, boxwood, or glued wood laminate. It is a medieval English bow with instinctive shooting that is only used by experienced archers who practice natural shooting. The longbow has a weight range of 25 to 60 pounds. For the competition, wood must be used to make the arrows.

Compound bow

The Compound is a bow that has a system of hoists that increases the power and speeds up the propulsion of the arrows. Size, length, and power are only a few of the many selection factors. The power of leisure bows can be as little as 12 lbs (for kids) or as high as 75 lbs (for hunting bows). The use of higher powers will be limited to hunting. On American design, it is mostly utilized for target shooting, hunting, and outdoor activities.

Barebow

The barebow is a classic recurve or longbow that may be shot without the use of accessories such a sight, stabilizer, range indicator, or aiming mark. It is used in some field shooting contests. All of the bow's decorations have been removed, perhaps with the exception of the button-berger.

Survival bow

Small, foldable longbows are essentially what survival bows are. This implies that they have far less power than a takedown recurve bow. However, the majority of survival bows can fire precisely up to 30 yards, which is more than sufficient for deer hunting, particularly in a survival situation.

The Japanese bow or Yumi

The Kuydo martial art, which is a legacy of the Samurai, is practiced with a traditional Japanese bow called the Yumi. Fiber or carbon, which were originally made of bamboo, are acceptable. The bow's asymmetrical handle is in the lower third and is only held with the left hand. The extremely long bow, which is over two meters long, is unequipped. The Ya is the name of the Japanese arrow. It can be made of carbon fiber, bamboo, or aluminum.

HOW TO PLAY

Archery Rules

• Two whistle blows: The archer moves to the shooting line.
• One whistle blow: The archer shoots.
• Three whistle blows: The archer retrieves their arrows.
• Each archer has two minutes to shoot three arrows.
• There are no re-shot’s allowed.
• Athletes can be penalized, disqualified, or even banned for breaching rules.
• Archer’s must wait until the signal to start is given before raising the bow arm.

Archery Skills and Techniques

There are common guidelines that archers adhere to, even though some techniques may vary, to help assure accuracy when shooting. These guidelines obviously do not apply to all forms of archery, but they will give you a general sense of how it works. Ready? Here we go, then!

Proper stance

Line up, so your feet are in a line towards the middle of the target. Your feet should be shoulder width apart, and your toes should be pointing at a 90-degree angle from the target. In other words, if you drew an imaginary line from the center of the target, it would hit the side of your foot.

Put the arrow in the bow

Put the arrow on the arrow rest, which is part of the bow. Place the bowstring into the nock, which is the slotted portion on the back of an arrow. Usually, the fletching, or the feather or plastic stabilizing portion of the bow, will have one that is odd colored, as you can see in the image below. Point the odd-colored fletch outward.

Grip the string

Typically, three fingers are used to hold the string. The pointer finger is held above the arrow, and the middle and ring finger are below the arrow. The grip should be loose.

Draw the bow & Aim

Raise the bow and draw, or pull the string back. Your bow arm, or the arm that is not drawing the string, should be pointed toward the target. Next, draw the string toward an anchor point. This varies depending on whether or not you're using a sight. Anchor points are typically the chin, corner of the mouth, or ear.
Some people aim with a sight, others do not.

Release

Let go of the string by relaxing your fingers. Don't jerk or move the bow after you have released the arrow. Stay in the stance until the arrow hits the target as to not move while the arrow is being released.

Scoring

Add up the number of points based on where the arrow hits the target. The lowest score you can receive for shooting one arrow is one point (hitting the outer white ring) while the highest score possible is ten points (hitting the inner gold ring).

Winning the Game

This will depend on the tournament style. During a competition, the winner is either the person who has the highest cumulative score after all the arrows have been shot, or the archer has outdueled his opponents in a “knockout” style game.

WHERE TO PLAY

Ace Archery

Ace Archery offers classes for all skill levels and is run by skilled coaches and trainers. Additionally, it plans events, projects, and activities relating to archery. Over 100 institutions, community centers, and schools have taken its courses so far.

Pasir Ris Elias Archery Club

If you live in the east and are interested in learning archery, the Pasir Ris Elias Archery Club is a laid-back group that offers enjoyable shooting sessions and archery lessons at the outdoor range. On some weekday evenings (ideal for the after-work crowd), these people hold regular shooting sessions, and they get going early on the weekends.

Flaming Arrows Archery Club

The Flaming Arrows Club, which was founded in 2005, specializes in beginner-level introductory classes. In Marsiling, there is a separate archery range. In addition to courses, you may schedule private sessions for families, businesses, or more in-depth classes on perfecting your form and execution (accessible to members only).

Salt & Light Archery

This location provides a pleasant family activity with courses ideal for all age groups, from four-year-olds to senior citizens. Beginner courses are available for those who wish to learn more about the sport, its fundamental regulations, and how to assemble a recurve bow.

The Archery Academy

The Archery Academy offers classes for all ability levels, complete with all the necessary equipment, whether you are an expert archer looking to improve your techniques or you are simply curious to give the sport a try. The 70-meter archery range offers a well-paved shooting line, cover from the sun and rain, and shade so you can shoot your bows there.

COMPETITIONS

Archery World Cup

Since the first edition of the Paralympic Games in 1960, archery has been a part of the Paralympic movement's history and has been on the schedule of every competition. Para-archery, which includes standing and wheelchair events, is available to competitors with physical disabilities in the Paralympic Games. Ten scoring zones are indicated on the target, and athletes shoot at it from a distance. Every two years, World Archery hosts the World Archery Para Championships, an international competition for para archers.

Olympic Archery

Target archery is practiced using recurve bows prior to the Olympics. Athletes engage in head-to-head competition to score set points—2 points for winning a set and 1 point for tying a set—firing at targets 70 meters away. The first player to score 6 set points wins the match. Olympic archery competitions are held in the form of the World Archery Field Championships and are held every two years. It was first hosted in 1969.

World Archery Championships

Every two years, World Archery hosts the World Archery Championships, an international competition for outdoor target archery. Each winner is referred to as the World Archery Champion, and it is regarded as the sport's pinnacle competition. In 1931, the event was first held.

World Archery 3D Championships

Shooting at fixed foam targets shaped like various animals that are placed around a course at various distances, heights, and angles is known as 3D archery. Every two years, World Archery hosts the World Archery 3D Championships, an international competition for 3D archers. The first one was held in 2003.

World Youth Championships (Outdoor Target)

Since 1991, a separate host city has hosted the World Youth Archery Championships, an annual international championship in archery. Recurve and compound bow competitions are held in the cadet and junior divisions.
